# Stakeholder Reporting Guide

Senior stakeholders need a concise answer to five questions: what changed, why it matters, how confident we are, what action is recommended, and what risk remains.

Use plain English before charts. Lead with commercial impact, customer impact, and the decision required. Avoid statistical jargon unless it changes the decision.

Good recommendations include owner, priority, expected benefit, and a follow-up measurement plan. If evidence is mixed, say what would increase confidence.

For product managers, translate insights into backlog decisions such as checkout friction fixes, segment targeting, instrumentation work, or experiment rollouts.
